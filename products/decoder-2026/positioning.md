# Positioning — 2026 Decoder Product Line

Competitive note for the launch, plus draft Etsy listing copy.

---

## The Etsy landscape, May 2026

Searched on Etsy for "NMJL 2026 mahjong card cheat sheet" and read the top ten listings. The market splits cleanly:

**Beginner glut ($3–$8).** The dominant product type. Visual reference cards that restate the printed NMJL card in color-coded categories — "Mahjong 2026 Printable Cheat Sheet, NMJL Card Hands Guide PDF, Beginner-friendly," and a dozen visually identical variants. Most are 1–4 pages. Most are visual translations of the official card with concealed hands stripped, point values stripped, and color groupings added. They sell because beginners are afraid of the official card; they do not sell on strategy.

Notable variants:
- **"Sequence Shortcuts"** — strips concealed hands and point values for a streamlined layout. Useful and honest. Still beginner positioning.
- **"Cutout cards"** — small printable reference cards for the table. A form-factor differentiation, not a content one.

**Strategic almost-empty ($8–$15+, mostly free as blogs).**
- **Salt Lake Mahjong Club** publishes a 3-page Pivot Cheat Sheet (the page-3 image the user shared) as part of a paid product. The data is dense and credible — built, per their footer, "on full AI analysis of 678 hand variants." Their voice is straightforward, slightly clinical. Their visual identity is plain.
- **bamcrak** publishes an analytical blog ("What to Hold, What to Pass & Where to Pivot"). Free. No PDF.
- **Modern Mahjong** sells a "Learn the 2026" PDF at a higher price point. Educational, not strategic.

**Where Prim sits.**  The strategic niche has data but no voice and no design. Prim has voice, design, and an audience. The Companion is the first strategic decoder for the 2026 card with a brand position above the category. The Primer covers the bottom of the funnel without playing the visual-restatement game.

## Pricing

- **The 2026 Primer:** **free** download from prim.orderofthetile.com, or **$3** Etsy listing for buyers who only know how to find the card on Etsy. The Etsy listing exists to capture searches, not to make money.
- **The 2026 Companion:** **$15.** Above the beginner band ($3–$8), at the top of the educational band ($8–$15), and below the typical "course" price point ($25+). $15 lets the listing read as serious without reading as overpriced. Round numbers feel less Etsy-native than $14.99 — Prim does not haggle.

Bundle: **$16 for both.** The bundle exists to give the Etsy listing a "starting at" price point and to make the Companion the obvious purchase.

## The launch sequence

1. Lay out both PDFs in InDesign from these drafts. Two weeks of design.
2. Newsletter announcement — one of Prim's regular Thursday letters, soft launch with a discount code for newsletter readers only ($12 Companion for the first week). The newsletter list is the warmest audience and should buy first.
3. Etsy listings go live the following Thursday. Beginner-glut keywords in the title; strategic keywords in the description. The listing photos are page mockups in Prim's palette, not screenshots of card data.
4. A second newsletter letter, two weeks after launch, on a different topic, with the Companion linked in the footer of the letter and on `the-game.html`. No second push email.
