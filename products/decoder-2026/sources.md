## Public analyses — external

### Salt Lake Mahjong Club — "The 2026 NMJL Card: What You Actually Need to Know"

URL: https://www.saltlakemahjongclub.com/blog/the-2026-nmjl-card-what-you-actually-need-to-know

Findings cited in the search snippet:
- "Pairs of hands sharing as many as 14 tiles in common — meaning you can often hold the same rack and be live on multiple hands well into the game."
- "Hand 4 (the 22 DD 222 666 NEWS hand) shares 12 or 13 tiles with multiple adjacent variants because the Winds (NEWS) and White Dragons are fixed while only the suit of your 2s and 6s changes."
- "The two Wind-anchor hands (NNNN EEE WWW SSSS and NNNN EEEE WWWW SSS) share 12 tiles — they're essentially the same hand with the E and W count swapped."
- "Based on full AI analysis of 678 hand variants" (visible on the page-3 footer of their Pivot Cheat Sheet, in the user's screenshot).

Their published 3-page Pivot Cheat Sheet structure (from screenshot of page 3):
- "Highest Overlap — 12 tiles in common" — one row.
- "11 tiles in common" — six rows.
- "10 tiles in common" — twenty-six rows.
- Sidebar: "Hold (most hands need these)" — `6s — 272 hands, 8 sections`, `Flowers — 238 hands`, `3s — 230 hands`, `2+6 together — 110 hands`, `3+9 together — 102 hands`, `5+7 together — 102 hands`.
- Sidebar: "Pass Early (fewest hands need these)" — `1s — only 137 hands (fewest number)`, `1+8 together — only 4 hands`, `2+9 together — only 4 hands`, `1+6 together — only 6 hands`, `Winds (unless WD, 13579-3, 369-4, 2026-4)`.

**Use rule:** The Companion may reference the *underlying truths* (e.g., the 6 is broadly useful; 1s appear in fewer hands; Winds are conditional) and may use Prim's own counts where they can be re-derived from public AMJA breakdowns. The Companion must **not** copy Salt Lake's row structure, their "12/11/10 tiles in common" tier breaks, or their phrasing.

## What is *not* sourceable here

The following claims would need additional verification before publication:
- Joker scarcity by section (Quints / Singles & Pairs are joker-restricted by NMJL rule, but the *exact* 2026 distribution needs the AMJA breakdown).
- Charleston pivot recommendations specific to individual hand families.
- Per-section permutation counts.

If the layout designer needs these for InDesign, source them against AMJA's published breakdown before adding to the draft. Don't infer.
