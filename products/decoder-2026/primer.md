### THE CARD HAS TEN SECTIONS.

Each section is a family of related hands. You play one hand per round. Picking your section early, and your hand inside the section by the end of the Charleston, is the whole game in miniature.

This year the sections are: 2026 (the year hand), 2468, 13579, 369, Any Like Numbers, Consecutive Run, Quints, Winds & Dragons, Singles & Pairs, and one further small grouping printed alongside the others. Quints have returned to a section of their own this year after a year's absence — that is the season's largest structural change.

### HOW TO READ A HAND.

Every line on the printed card shows you a hand: the tile pattern, the score it is worth, and a marker telling you whether the hand is *exposed* (you may show parts of it during play) or *concealed* (you must keep all of it hidden until the win). The "X" or "C" letters next to a hand tell you which.

Start by reading only the patterns. Ignore the scores until you can hold the patterns in your head; they will not help you choose a hand if you do not yet know what the hands look like.

### WHAT A NEW PLAYER GETS WRONG, GENTLY.

**The 1.**  New players hold 1s out of habit. The 1 is the narrowest tile on this year's card. Pass yours unless you are committed to a hand that needs them.

**Flowers.**  This year's card is flower-heavy. Do not pass flowers in the first Charleston. *Treat them as currency.*

**Jokers.**  Hold them. The temptation to play a joker into a kong you could finish naturally is the most expensive mistake in the game. Wait two more pulls before deciding.

## End notes for the InDesign typesetter

- **One page only.** If the page overflows, cut copy. Do not spill onto a second page — the discipline of one page is part of the funnel's promise.
- **No emojis. No exclamation points.** The Primer is gentle but not cute.
- **The closing block** is the entire reason this product exists. Make it visually distinct without making it feel like an ad — Cloud panel, Lilac hairline border, Melon for the headline only. The body of the block should still feel like Prim writing, not a marketing call-out.
- **Type stack and palette:** identical to the Companion (`companion.md` end notes). The two products read as a set.
