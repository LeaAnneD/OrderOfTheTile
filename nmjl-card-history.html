RESEARCH ARCHIVE

The Card Has a History.

A reference archive of the National Mah Jongg League annual card, from the founding in 1937 to today. Every year tells a story about the game we love.

Important Research Note: The NMJL treats its annual card as proprietary. NMJL has never officially published an archive of historical hands. This document contains: (1) complete, verified hand-level data for 2025–2026 sourced from public breakdowns by the American Mah Jongg Association; (2) partial documented hands for 2022–2024 from community analyses; and (3) structural/historical notes for 1937–2021 based on documented evolution. For vintage cards (1940–2025), reproduction cards are available commercially from MahjForAll.org.

The Founding Era · 1937–1949

1937
FOUNDING YEAR No Jokers ~6 Flowers
▼
✦ The First Card — NMJL Founded in New York City
The National Mah Jongg League was founded in 1937 by a group of Jewish women in New York City who wanted to standardize the game. The first card established the framework that continues today: a laminated sheet of winning hands grouped into categories, updated annually. Early cards had no Jokers and far fewer flowers than modern cards. The exact hands are not publicly documented, but they featured simpler pung/kong structures across suits without the year-hand system introduced later.

1940–1949
0–4 Jokers 6–12 Flowers WWII Era
▼
Cards from this decade show gradual evolution of the tile set. Joker tiles were introduced progressively — early 1940s cards used 0–2 Jokers; by the late 1940s some cards included up to 4. The number of Flower tiles varied between 6 and 12. MahjForAll has reproduced all individual year cards (1940–1949) based on original sources. Exact hand compositions differ year to year; complete hand lists for these years require purchasing the reproduction cards.

Mid-Century Era · 1950–1979

1950–1953
4–6 Jokers No Year Hands Yet
▼
The early 1950s continued refining the card format. The number of Jokers stabilized toward 6–8. Flowers expanded. MahjForAll reproduces all years 1950–1953 individually. Exact hand lists require the reproduction cards.
Source: MahjForAll archive
1954
YEAR HANDS DEBUT Referenced Prior Year
▼
✦ Year Hands Introduced — But Referenced the Previous Year Until 1961
1954 is the first year the NMJL card included "Year Hands" — hands built around the digits of the current year. However, the year referenced on the card did not align with the actual play year until 1961. So a card used in 1954 might reference 1953. This quirk is well-documented by NMJL historians and MahjForAll.
Year Hands (debut)
—
Year digits used to build pungs, kongs, pairs
Soap (White Dragon) represents 0. Referenced prior year until 1961 alignment.
Source: MahjForAll product notes; NMJL history documentation
1955–1960
Year Hands (misaligned) 6–8 Jokers
▼
Cards through 1960 continued with Year Hands referencing the prior calendar year. The 1960 card did not include a standard Year Hand because the year contains a zero and zero tiles (Soap as zero) were not standardized until 1980. Some cards from this era used creative workarounds or historical themes for year-like sections.
Source: MahjForAll archival notes; individual reproduction cards available
1961
YEAR ALIGNMENT 8 Jokers Standard
▼
✦ Year Hands Finally Aligned with Actual Calendar Year
1961 marks the year when the Year section of the card finally matched the actual year being played. This alignment has been maintained ever since (except for years containing zero digits, handled by Soap tiles). The 8-Joker set also became standardized around this era.
Source: MahjForAll archival notes
1962–1979
8 Jokers 8 Flowers No Zero Tiles
▼
Through the 1960s and 70s, the modern NMJL card structure solidified. Eight Jokers and eight Flowers became standard. The major outstanding issue: years containing a zero digit (1960, 1970) had no clear tile representation for "0" — that wouldn't be solved until 1980. Cards from 1970 either skipped Year Hands or used alternative number arrangements. MahjForAll reproduces all years in this range.

Modern Era · 1980–2014

1980
ZERO TILE DEBUT Soap = 0
▼
✦ White Dragon (Soap) Officially Adopted as Zero — First Zero-Year Card
1980 introduced the Soap tile (White Dragon) as the standard stand-in for zero on Year Hands. This solved the longstanding problem of years containing "0" and became a permanent rule. The 1980 card was the first to use this convention, which remains in use today (2020, 2026, etc.).
Source: MahjForAll "Did You Know?" archival notes
1981–1998
8 Jokers 8 Flowers Soap = Zero Standard
▼
These years represent the classic NMJL card format in its mature state. The structure of sections was well-established. Exact hand compositions changed annually. MahjForAll reproduces all individual years through this period. The community began growing significantly during the 1990s, and card analysis culture (comparing year-to-year changes) started informally.

Note: The 2025 card analysis referenced a specific CR/7 hand that "originally appeared on a NMJL card back in the 90s" — confirming that some hands recur across decades.
Source: MahjForAll archive; ILoveMahj 2025 analysis (citing 1990s hands)
1999
FIRST ALT CARD Alternative Card Debuts
▼
✦ First Known Alternative Card Released (non-NMJL)
1999 saw the first known alternative American Mah Jongg card published by a third party. These alternative cards use the NMJL framework (three suits, jokers, Charleston, flowers, 14-tile structure) but introduce new hands and rules. Gladys Grad's Siamese/Tournament card is the most prominent alternative, notable for creative hands not typically seen on the NMJL card. Several independent publishers now offer alternatives.
Source: Mahj Life, "American Mah Jongg Article 162"
2000–2014
8 Jokers · 8 Flowers Community Growth
▼
The 2000s brought rapid growth in American Mah Jongg. Annual card analysis started appearing online. The NMJL grew from a few thousand members to hundreds of thousands. The card format remained consistent but hands evolved annually. The year section continued using Soap for zeros (2000, 2001, 2002, 2010 cards all required zero representations). Addition and multiplication hands were introduced during this era as a section novelty. MahjForAll reproduces all years 2000–2025.

Recent Era · 2015–2021

2015–2021
8 Jokers · 8 Flowers 350,000+ members Online Analysis Era
▼
This period saw the emergence of sophisticated annual card analysis from sites like ILoveMahj, The Mahjong Line, and others. Community hand tracking became formalized. Cards from 2015 onward were analyzed for hand counts, tile demand, difficulty ratings, and cross-year comparisons. The NMJL reached 350,000+ members.

Key structural data (from Bam Good Time's 11-year analysis of 2015–2025 cards): total printed hands ranged from approximately 66 to 73 per year; concealed hands comprised roughly 14–16% of printed hands; point values ranged 25–75 (35-point hands were removed sometime in this period and not restored).

Current Era · 2022–2026 · Fully Documented

2022
~66 Hands 3 Twos in Year Joker Rule Note Added
▼
The 2022 card had an unusual year composition: three 2s and two 2s (for "2022"), creating high demand for 2-tiles. The NMJL added a note directly on the front of the card: "JOKERS MAY NOT BE USED IN A 2022 GROUPING" to clarify that the four-digit year grouping uses singles, not a kong. Approximately 66 printed hands (fewer than most recent years). The card prompted significant community confusion around the year hand joker rule, which carried forward into 2023 (the note was eventually removed in 2023 as causing more confusion than it solved).

2025
71 Hands 1,002 Permutations No Quints Section
▼
The 2025 card contains 71 printed hands representing 1,002 permutations. Approximately 10 hands are concealed. Nine sections appear on the card (No Quints section this year). Educational material follows to help players understand the structure and rules of each section, complete with SVG tile sample solutions.
2025 Year & W&D Section
What this section is: Year Hands use the digits of 2025 to build pungs (3-of-a-kind), kongs (4-of-a-kind), or pairs. Soap (White Dragon) represents 0. The unique arrangement of the year digits creates a specific pattern. Winds & Dragons hands use only winds (N, E, S, W), dragons (Red, Green, White/Soap), and flowers as combinations.
Key rules:
  • The year grouping often uses singles or small melds built from 2-0-2-5 digits
  • Soap tile (White Dragon) always represents zero on year hands
  • Winds are N, E, S, W; Dragons are R (Red), G (Green), 0/Soap (White)
  • Flowers are suitless and can pair with any hand
Sample solution:
The singles spell 2025 (Soap=0). The pung uses the first year digit in one suit; the kong uses the second year digit in a different suit. Three suits total.
2025 Evens (2-4-6-8)
What this section is: All numbered tiles in the hand are even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8) across the three suits. Flowers and dragons are typically included. Jokers can be used freely.
Key rules:
  • All numbered tiles must be even: 2, 4, 6, or 8 only
  • No 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9 numbered tiles allowed
  • Flowers, winds, and dragons can be used without restriction
  • Jokers substitute for any tile in the hand
Sample solution:
Two Flowers (suitless), a kong of 6-Bam, pairs of 2-Bam and 4-Crak, and a kong of 8-Crak. Two suits.
2025 Any Like Numbers
What this section is: Three or four melds (groups) where each group consists of the same number in different suits. For example, three pungs of 7 (one 7-Bam, one 7-Crak, one 7-Dot) plus a pair. The number can be any 1–9.
Key rules:
  • Pick any number from 1–9
  • Use that number across different suits in pungs, kongs, or pairs
  • Typically three suit-groups (all the same number, each in a different suit)
  • Flowers and dragons appear as additional melds or pairs
  • Jokers can substitute within the number-melds
Sample solution:
All three suit-pungs use 7. Green Dragons (any dragon is valid). Two Flowers.
2025 Quint-Style (Embedded Example)
What this section is: Quint hands are 5-of-a-kind melds (requiring exactly one Joker per quint). These appear as a structural variant rather than a full section. Hands may feature one or more quints combined with pairs and smaller melds.
Key rules:
  • A quint = 4 tiles of the same number + 1 Joker (must be used this way)
  • Each quint requires exactly one Joker
  • Quints can appear in different suits or combined with winds/dragons
  • Remaining melds follow standard pung-kong-pair rules
Sample solution:
Consecutive run of 4-5-6 in Dots. The quint of 6 uses one Joker.
2025 Consecutive Run
What this section is: A sequence of consecutive numbers (like 1-2-3-4-5 or 3-4-5-6-7) all in the same suit, grouped into melds. The entire run can span one or multiple melds and suits.
Key rules:
  • Numbers must be consecutive: 1-2-3-4-5, 2-3-4-5-6, etc.
  • The run can be split across multiple melds (e.g., a pair of 1, then pung of 2-3-4)
  • Flowers are typically included as a separate pair
  • Jokers can substitute within the run
Sample solution:
Three Flowers, then two full five-number runs in different suits. No jokers anywhere — pure wall hand.
2025 Odds (1-3-5-7-9)
What this section is: All numbered tiles must be odd (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) across the three suits. Flowers and dragons are included. Jokers can be used freely.
Key rules:
  • All numbered tiles must be odd: 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9 only
  • No even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8) allowed
  • Flowers, winds, and dragons can be used without restriction
  • Jokers substitute for any tile
Sample solution:
Alternating pungs and kongs of odd numbers. Three suits required. No flowers on this one.
2025 Winds & Dragons
What this section is: Hands composed only of winds (N, E, S, W), dragons (Red, Green, White/Soap), and flowers. No numbered tiles at all.
Key rules:
  • Only winds (N, E, S, W), dragons (R/Red, G/Green, 0/Soap), and flowers
  • No numbered tiles from any suit
  • Jokers can be used to complete pungs, kongs, or pairs
  • Flowers are typically a pair or small grouping
Sample solution:
Four wind pairs + two dragon pungs. All concealed. No jokers in pairs.
2025 3-6-9
What this section is: Hands using only tiles numbered 3, 6, or 9 (plus flowers and dragons). The number set creates a restricted palette of exactly 9 numeric tiles across all suits.
Key rules:
  • Numbered tiles are limited to 3, 6, and 9 only
  • These numbers appear across all three suits (Bam, Crak, Dot)
  • Flowers and dragons can be used freely
  • Jokers substitute within the 3-6-9 palette
Sample solution:
Flowers + pung of 3 + kong of 6 + kong of 9. Two suits.
2025 Singles & Pairs
What this section is: Hands where all 14 tiles are either single tiles (appearing once) or pairs (two identical tiles). No pungs, kongs, or melds — and crucially, no Jokers allowed. These are typically the highest-point hands on the card.
Key rules:
  • All 14 tiles must be singles or pairs only
  • NO Jokers permitted in this section
  • Maximum of one pair at a time (since 14 tiles = 7 pairs, or mix of singles and pairs totaling 14)
  • Flowers can appear as part of the singles or as a pair
  • Typically worth 25–75 points (highest-value hands)
Sample solution:
Eight pairs — four evens in Bam, four evens in Crak. All concealed, no jokers.
2025 Big Hand (Special)
What this section is: A unique special hand built around the year digits and flowers. This is a showcase hand that demonstrates the beauty of the year pattern combined with flowers and a specific structure.
Key rules:
  • Year digits form the core of the hand (2-0-2-5 in 2025)
  • Flowers are often featured prominently
  • The pattern must use all tiles as singles or in small melds
  • No Jokers typically allowed, demonstrating "pure" play
Sample solution:
Two Flowers + three repetitions of the 2025 digit singles, one per suit. All 16 tiles are singles or the Flower pair — no jokers anywhere.
Source: American Mah Jongg Association public breakdowns; ILoveMahj 2025; Modern Mahjong; community analysis
2026
69 Hands 1,458 Permutations Quints Return
▼
The 2026 card features 69 printed hands representing 1,458 permutations. Quints section returns after a year's absence. The card is characterized by heavy Flower content (including a sextet of Flowers — 6-of-a-kind), a pung-kong dominant structure, and year digits split across multiple suits. Approximately 11 hands are concealed. Ten sections appear on the card.
2026 Year Section
What this section is: Year Hands in 2026 use the digits 2-0-2-6 (where Soap = 0). The distinctive feature of 2026: an extremely heavy Flower presence, including a potential sextet (6 Flowers). Year digits are distributed across different suits, creating multiple melds.
Key rules:
  • Year digits form the core (2-0-2-6); Soap represents 0
  • Flowers appear in unusual quantities (up to 6 in a single hand)
  • Year digits may be split across two or three suits
  • Winds & Dragons can combine with year digits
Sample solution:
Six Flowers (a sextet!) plus year digits 2-0-2-6 split across two suits. This illustrates the heavy flower + year digit structure.
2026 Evens (2-4-6-8)
What this section is: All numbered tiles are even (2, 4, 6, 8). Flowers and dragons are included. The 2026 card emphasizes pungs and kongs over pairs in this section.
Key rules:
  • Only even numbers: 2, 4, 6, 8
  • Flowers and dragons add to the hand
  • Jokers can be used freely
Sample solution:
Three Flowers + kong of 2-Bam + kong of 4-Crak + pung of 6-Dot. Three suits, heavy pung-kong structure.
2026 Any Like Numbers
What this section is: Three or four melds (same number across different suits). 2026 features multiple Flowers plus the like-number structure.
Key rules:
  • Same number across different suits
  • Flowers and dragons can be part of the hand
  • Jokers substitute within the number-melds
Sample solution:
Four Flowers + kong of 5-Bam + pung of 5-Crak + pung of 5-Dot. Same number throughout.
2026 Quints (Returned)
What this section is: Quints return for 2026! These are 5-of-a-kind melds (4 tiles + 1 Joker). Quints hands are highly prized for their complexity.
Key rules:
  • Quint = 4 identical tiles + 1 Joker (must be exact)
  • Each quint requires one Joker
  • Can appear in different suits or with winds/dragons
  • Remaining melds are standard pungs, kongs, pairs
Sample solution:
Two Flowers + quint of 3-Bam (using 1 Joker) + quint of 3-Crak (using 1 Joker) + pair of 3-Dot. Quints section returns for 2026.
2026 Consecutive Run
What this section is: Consecutive numbers (like 1-2-3-4-5) split into melds. 2026 features all three suits represented in the run, plus wind/dragon elements.
Key rules:
  • Numbers are consecutive
  • Can span multiple melds and suits
  • Flowers are typically a separate pair
Sample solution:
Two Flowers + run of 1-2-3 in all three suits + pung of Red Dragons. Compact, colorful hand.
2026 Odds (1-3-5-7-9)
What this section is: All numbered tiles are odd (1, 3, 5, 7, 9). The 2026 card emphasizes kongs and pungs in this section.
Key rules:
  • Only odd numbers: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9
  • Flowers and dragons can be used freely
Sample solution:
Kong of 1-Bam + pung of 3-Crak + pung of 5-Dot + kong of 7-Bam. Three suits, pung-kong structure.
2026 Winds & Dragons
What this section is: Hands composed only of winds, dragons, and flowers. No numbered tiles. 2026 features a simple classic pattern.
Key rules:
  • Only winds, dragons, and flowers
  • No numbered tiles
Sample solution:
Four wind pungs + pair of Red Dragons. Classic W&D structure.
2026 3-6-9
What this section is: Only tiles numbered 3, 6, or 9. The 2026 card continues the pung-kong emphasis.
Key rules:
  • Numbers limited to 3, 6, 9 only
  • Across all three suits
  • Flowers and dragons included
Sample solution:
Three Flowers + kong of 3 + pung of 6 + kong of 9. Three suits.
2026 Singles & Pairs
What this section is: All 14 tiles are singles or pairs, with no Jokers allowed. The 2026 card uses year digits in this section, combined with flowers and other elements.
Key rules:
  • 14 tiles: all singles or pairs
  • NO Jokers
  • Year digits or other special combinations
Sample solution:
Year digits 2-0-2-6 in three suits (12 singles) + pair of Flowers. All concealed, no jokers.
